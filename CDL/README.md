# Clarity Definition Layer (CDL)

The Clarity Definition Layer (CDL) is the regulatory backbone of the tri-architecture 
ecosystem. It provides structural clarity, boundary enforcement, and definition 
stability for all downstream and upstream systems, including NDH conceptual 
architecture and the Chill Dispatch Center.

CDL ensures that conceptual expansion, nonsense traversal, and dispatch operations 
remain coherent, safe, and aligned with the system’s clarity-first principles.

CDL is the layer where definitions stabilize, boundaries form, and architectural 
integrity is maintained.

---

## Purpose

The Clarity Definition Layer exists to:

- Provide stable definitions for multi-layer architectures
- Maintain regulatory clarity across conceptual and nonsense subsystems
- Anchor NDH conceptual expansion to safe, interpretable boundaries
- Support Chill Dispatch routing and cooling operations
- Prevent ambiguity from destabilizing system behavior
- Ensure all subsystems operate within clarity-aligned constraints

CDL is not a creative layer.  
CDL is not a nonsense layer.  
CDL is a **regulatory clarity layer**.

## Regulatory Functions

CDL provides:

- **Definition Stability**  
  Ensures terms, modules, and subsystems maintain consistent meaning.

- **Boundary Enforcement**  
  Prevents conceptual or nonsense overflow into unsafe zones.

- **Clarity Anchoring**  
  Grounds NDH conceptual structures in interpretable definitions.

- **Subsystem Containment**  
  Ensures BS remains nonsense, NDH remains conceptual, and CDC remains operational.

- **Cross-Layer Alignment**  
  Maintains coherence between conceptual, regulatory, and nonsense layers.
