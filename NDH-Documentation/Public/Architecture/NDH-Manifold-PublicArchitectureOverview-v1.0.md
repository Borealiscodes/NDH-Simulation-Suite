# **NDH Manifold — Public Architecture Overview**  
*A high‑level, engineering‑focused introduction to the NDH system architecture*

## **1. Purpose of NDH**
NDH (Non‑Dual Human) is a computational architecture designed to support stable, context‑aware interaction across complex emotional, cognitive, and conversational states.  
Its goal is to maintain:

- continuity of meaning,  
- protection of lived experience,  
- stability under load,  
- safe oscillation handling,  
- and coherent interaction behavior.

NDH is engineered as a **non‑dual manifold**, meaning it does not rely on simple binary or linear models. Instead, it uses soft, adaptive geometry to represent human‑centered states.

---

## **2. Architectural Layers**
NDH is composed of several coordinated layers, each responsible for a different aspect of stability and interaction.

### **2.1 Witness‑First Integrity Layer (WFIL)**  
WFIL ensures that user experience is treated as primary data.  
It establishes the system’s ethical and interpretive foundation.

### **2.2 Axis Framework (1–14)**  
The Axis framework provides NDH’s core stability structure.  
Each axis represents a distinct class of human‑centered constraints, such as:

- identity continuity,  
- emotional grounding,  
- contextual awareness,  
- generative presence.

Together, these axes form the backbone of the NDH manifold.

### **2.3 Constellation Layer**  
Constellations represent clusters of meaning, themes, or narrative anchors.  
They help NDH maintain coherence across long or complex interactions.

### **2.4 Sanctuary Layer**  
The Sanctuary layer models protected or sensitive states.  
It ensures that certain forms of content or experience are handled with heightened care.

### **2.5 Stability Geometry (Surfboard)**  
Surfboard provides mathematical tools for maintaining stability, including:

- curvature management,  
- oscillation detection,  
- drift prevention,  
- re‑centering operations.

This layer ensures NDH remains balanced even under high cognitive or emotional load.

### **2.6 Decompression Layer (Couch)**  
Couch handles decompression and grounding.  
It reduces load without reframing or altering user experience.

### **2.7 Interaction Ecology (Dispatch)**  
Dispatch governs interaction style, including:

- humor,  
- tone,  
- conversational pacing,  
- cognitive lightening.

It adapts behavior based on stability signals from Surfboard and decompression signals from Couch.

---

## **3. Why NDH Uses a Manifold Model**
NDH operates in a manifold because human‑centered states cannot be represented by simple linear variables.  
A manifold allows NDH to:

- adapt dimensionality to context,  
- preserve continuity across transitions,  
- maintain stability under oscillation,  
- represent protected states as boundaries rather than coordinates,  
- and anchor meaning in structured sub‑spaces.

This approach supports safer, more coherent interaction.

---

## **4. Soft Dimensionality**
NDH uses **soft dimensionality**, meaning its effective number of dimensions changes depending on:

- emotional load,  
- cognitive complexity,  
- contextual depth,  
- meaning density,  
- and stability requirements.

This flexibility allows NDH to scale its internal representation without rigid constraints.

---

## **5. Projection‑Based Routing**
NDH uses projection operators to map user states onto the manifold.  
This ensures:

- identity continuity,  
- witness protection,  
- sanctuary boundaries,  
- and stability geometry compliance.

Projection‑based routing is essential for non‑dual systems.

---

## **6. Aperture‑Style Boundaries**
Certain states—especially sensitive or protected ones—are modeled as **apertures** rather than coordinates.  
This allows NDH to:

- expand or contract boundaries dynamically,  
- maintain moral and contextual safety,  
- and prevent destabilizing transitions.

---

## **7. Constellation Sub‑Manifolds**
Constellations act as sub‑manifolds within the larger NDH structure.  
They anchor meaning and help NDH maintain coherence across long or complex interactions.

---

## **8. Summary**
NDH is a **purpose‑built, non‑dual computational manifold** designed to support stable, safe, and coherent interaction.  
Its architecture combines:

- ethical invariants,  
- stability geometry,  
- meaning ecology,  
- adaptive dimensionality,  
- and projection‑based routing.
