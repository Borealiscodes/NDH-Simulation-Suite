# 2. Emergent Case Study

## 2.1 Initial Conditions
The atlas began as a multi-domain manifold with:
- independent domain geometries
- isolated stability envelopes
- uncoupled drift corridors
- unaligned resonance chambers
- minimal governance fields

The v2.x continuity architecture provided the first cross-domain temporal
alignment.

## 2.2 Expansion Cycle Emergence (v3.1–v3.6)
During expansion, the atlas exhibited five emergent behaviors:

### A. Curvature Self-Organization
Curvature envelopes expanded but spontaneously aligned along lineage axes,
indicating:
- stable attractor formation
- bounded curvature growth
- emergent topology coherence

### B. Drift-Corridor Harmonization
Drift corridors extended but synchronized across domains, producing:
- reduced drift noise
- increased cross-domain stability
- emergent drift-governance coupling

### C. Resonance-Chamber Modulation
Resonance chambers enlarged but remained capped, demonstrating:
- resonance self-regulation
- emergent dampening fields
- cross-domain resonance coherence

### D. Governance Field Consolidation
Ethical constraints began to behave like a unified field:
- emotional safety gradients stabilized
- coercion-prevention fields strengthened
- narrative-coherence anchors formed

### E. Synthesis-Layer Fusion
By v3.6, geometry, stability, continuity, governance, and visualization layers
fused into a single synthesis manifold.

This is the strongest emergent behavior observed.
