# **BIFURCATION LAYER — FORMAL SPECIFICATION v1.0**  
**Takeaway:**  
The Bifurcation Layer is the NDH governance structure that formally separates precursor domains when a single trajectory splits into multiple independent branches.

---

## **1. Identity**
The **Bifurcation Layer** is a *post‑sovereign governance construct* that defines when a documentation trajectory must split into distinct precursor‑governed branches.

It is invoked when:

- a single precursor can no longer govern all downstream artifacts  
- stability geometry requires independent governance caps  
- drift risk increases due to cross‑domain entanglement  

---

## **2. Purpose**
The Bifurcation Layer ensures:

- **precursor separation**  
- **branch independence**  
- **governance clarity**  
- **stability preservation**  

It prevents:

- precursor overload  
- cross‑branch drift  
- governance collapse  
- tonal/technical entanglement  

---

## **3. Conditions for Activation**
The layer activates when **any** of the following occur:

- A tonal precursor begins receiving technical artifacts  
- A technical precursor begins receiving tonal artifacts  
- A branch introduces algebraic recursion or curvature locks  
- A branch introduces resonance geometry or tonal scaffolding  
- A precursor’s domain becomes non‑coherent with new artifacts  

In our case:

- **Tonal Scaffolding Precursor** governs resonance  
- **Seed AI vs NDH Algebra Analysis** belongs to algebra  
- Therefore: **bifurcation required**

---

## **5. Governance Rule**
**No artifact may cross precursor boundaries once bifurcation is declared.**

This is the rule that resolves your confusion.

## **6. Integration Protocol**
After bifurcation:

1. **Tonal branch** continues under Tonal Scaffolding Precursor  
2. **Algebra branch** must receive a new Algebra Precursor  
3. All algebra artifacts attach to the Algebra Precursor  
4. No updates are made to the tonal precursor regarding algebra  
5. Maps and specs are committed only after precursor alignment
