# **NDH Domain Ecology Spec**  
*Formation, evolution, interaction, collapse, and inheritance of domains within the 50D NDH manifold*

---

## **1. Purpose**

The NDH Domain Ecology layer defines how **domains** emerge, evolve, interact, stabilize, collapse, and propagate across the NDH manifold.  
It is the ecological layer sitting directly above the **Runtime Kernel** and **Stability Geometry**.

Domains are NDH’s equivalent of:

- ecosystems  
- regions  
- semantic biomes  
- stability basins  
- resonance corridors  
- paradox fault zones  

They are **emergent structures**, not manually defined.

---

# **2. Domain Ontology**

Domains are defined as connected regions \(U \subset \mathcal{M}_{50}\) where:

\[
\text{DomainSignature}(U) = \arg\max \left( \sigma, \mathcal{R}, \mathcal{P}, \lambda, A, k \right)
\]

A domain is characterized by:

- **Field signature** (dominant serenity/resonance/paradox)  
- **Geometry signature** (curvature, metric distortion)  
- **Semantic signature** (cuil level, operator density)  
- **Appateur signature** (activation type, channel density)  
- **Stability signature** (attractor/corridor/collapse/overload)  

Domains are **typed** based on these signatures.

---

# **3. Domain Types**

### **3.1 Serenity Domains**
- High \(\sigma\)  
- Low \(\mathcal{R}\), low \(\mathcal{P}\)  
- Geometry: flattened, low curvature  
- Behavior: stable, slow evolution  
- Often form **attractor basins**

### **3.2 Resonance Domains**
- High \(\|\mathcal{R}\|\)  
- Geometry: stretched, filamentary  
- Behavior: fast evolution, operator‑dense  
- Often form **runaway corridors**

### **3.3 Paradox Domains**
- High \(\mathcal{P}\)  
- Geometry: spiked curvature, unstable  
- Behavior: chaotic, appateur‑hyperactive  
- Often form **collapse zones**

### **3.4 Stability Domains**
- High \(\lambda\)  
- Geometry: layered, basin‑like  
- Behavior: regulatory, damping  
- Often form **governance centers**

### **3.5 Appateur Domains**
- High \(A(x)\)  
- Geometry: portal‑dense  
- Behavior: non‑local, cross‑axiom  
- Often form **semantic hubs**

### **3.6 Mixed Domains**
- No single dominant field  
- Behavior: transitional, drifting  
- Often form **ecological boundaries**

---

# **4. Domain Formation**

Domains form when local field gradients exceed a threshold:

\[
\|\nabla S_t\| > \theta_{\text{domain}}
\]

Where \(S_t\) is the full state vector.

### **4.1 Formation mechanisms**

Domains emerge through:

- **Field differentiation** (serenity vs resonance vs paradox)  
- **Geometry bifurcation** (curvature changes)  
- **Appateur clustering** (portal density)  
- **Operator accumulation** (semantic load)  
- **Stability basin formation** (λ‑driven attractors)

### **4.2 Formation stages**

1. **Proto‑domain:** weak gradients, unstable boundaries  
2. **Nascent domain:** stable signature emerges  
3. **Mature domain:** stable geometry + field identity  
4. **Evolving domain:** drift, expansion, contraction  
5. **Terminal domain:** collapse or absorption

---

# **5. Domain Boundaries**

Domain boundaries are defined by:

\[
\partial U = \{ x : \|\nabla S_t(x)\| = \theta_{\text{boundary}} \}
\]

### **5.1 Boundary types**

- **Soft boundaries:** gradual transitions  
- **Hard boundaries:** sharp field discontinuities  
- **Fractured boundaries:** paradox‑driven instability  
- **Permeable boundaries:** appateur‑mediated flow  
- **Cross‑axiom boundaries:** transitions between \(\mathcal{S}_\alpha\)

### **5.2 Boundary dynamics**

Boundaries:

- drift  
- merge  
- fracture  
- dissolve  
- invert  
- become channels  

---

# **6. Domain Interaction Geometry**

Domains interact through:

- **field flow**  
- **operator propagation**  
- **appateur channels**  
- **stability gradients**  
- **cross‑axiom coupling**

### **6.1 Interaction types**

- **Serenity ↔ Resonance:** damping vs amplification  
- **Resonance ↔ Paradox:** runaway vs collapse  
- **Serenity ↔ Paradox:** suppression vs destabilization  
- **Appateur ↔ Any:** non‑local influence  
- **Stability ↔ Any:** regulation vs drift

### **6.2 Interaction outcomes**

- fusion  
- absorption  
- repulsion  
- collapse  
- bifurcation  
- inheritance  

---

# **7. Domain Drift**

Domains drift when:

\[
\frac{d}{dt} \text{DomainSignature}(U) \neq 0
\]

### **7.1 Drift drivers**

- field evolution  
- geometry evolution  
- operator activity  
- appateur activation  
- stability changes  
- channel formation  

### **7.2 Drift modes**

- **Linear drift:** slow, directional  
- **Chaotic drift:** paradox‑driven  
- **Oscillatory drift:** resonance‑driven  
- **Collapse drift:** stability loss  
- **Cross‑axiom drift:** semantic migration

---

# **8. Domain Collapse**

Domains collapse when:

\[
A_{\mathcal{P}}(x) > \theta_{\mathcal{P}}
\quad\text{or}\quad
\Lambda(U) < 0
\]

### **8.1 Collapse signatures**

- curvature spikes  
- appateur hyperactivation  
- channel overload  
- operator cascades  
- semantic instability (cuil spikes)

### **8.2 Collapse outcomes**

- fragmentation  
- absorption  
- annihilation  
- inversion  
- paradox bloom  
- appateur storm

---

# **9. Domain Inheritance**

Collapsed domains leave **inheritance residues**:

- field residues  
- geometric residues  
- operator residues  
- appateur residues  
- stability residues  

These residues seed **new proto‑domains**.

This is NDH’s ecological regeneration mechanism.

The NDH Domain Ecology layer:

- defines domain formation

- defines boundaries

- defines drift  
- defines collapse  
- defines inheritance  

It is the **world‑building layer** of NDH’s 50D semantic–geometric ecology.
