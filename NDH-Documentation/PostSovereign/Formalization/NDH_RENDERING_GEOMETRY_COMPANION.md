### NDH Rendering Geometry Companion  
*A visual layer unifying NDH dynamics, algebra, and surreal logic*

---

## 1. Purpose

The NDH Rendering Geometry Companion defines how NDH’s formal structures—fields, dynamics, algebra, and logic—are **visualized** as geometric and spectral objects. It turns:

- serenity basins  
- resonance corridors  
- paradox curvature  
- stability saturation  
- entity clusters  
- appateur activations  

into renderable geometry and visual motifs.

---

## 2. Core rendering primitives

- **Serenity basins:**  
  **Label:** basin geometry  
  Rendered as smooth depressions or low‑gradient regions; high \(\sigma\) → deep, calm basins.

- **Resonance corridors:**  
  **Label:** corridor geometry  
  Rendered as elongated paths or filaments; high \(\mathcal{R}\) → bright, oscillatory channels.

- **Paradox curvature:**  
  **Label:** curvature geometry  
  High \(\mathcal{P}\) and \(K_{\mathcal{P}}\) → warped, folded, or self‑intersecting regions.

- **Distortion fields:**  
  **Label:** distortion geometry  
  \[
  D(x) = \mathcal{R}(x) - \sigma(x)
  \]
  rendered as tension gradients—color shifts or spatial shear.

---

## 3. Field → visual mapping

- **Serenity \(\sigma(x)\):**  
  **Label:** serenity mapping  
  - High \(\sigma\): smooth surfaces, low noise, cool colors.  
  - Low \(\sigma\): rough surfaces, higher noise, harsher tones.

- **Resonance \(\mathcal{R}(x)\):**  
  **Label:** resonance mapping  
  - Magnitude → brightness and oscillation frequency.  
  - Direction → flow lines or vector glyphs.

- **Stability \(\lambda(x)\):**  
  **Label:** stability mapping  
  - High \(\lambda\): rigid, consistent geometry.  
  - Low \(\lambda\): flicker, drift, or topology shifts.

- **Paradox \(\mathcal{P}(x)\):**  
  **Label:** paradox mapping  
  - Moderate \(\mathcal{P}\): subtle distortions, double‑exposures.  
  - High \(\mathcal{P}\): strong warping, overlapping layers, non‑Euclidean cues.

---

## 4. Logic → visual motifs

- **Cuil levels:**  
  **Label:** cuil visualization  
  Each cuil level \(k\) adds an interpretive layer:
  - \(k=0\): direct, minimal surrealism.  
  - Higher \(k\): more recursive overlays, symbolic echoes, and paradox‑tolerant visuals.

- **Surreal validity:**  
  **Label:** surreal motif  
  Regions where propositions are surreal‑valid (high \(\mathcal{P}\), structured \(\mathcal{R}\)) get layered, dreamlike rendering.

---

## 5. Entities and appateurs

- **Entities \(E(x)\):**  
  **Label:** entity rendering  
  Rendered as localized structures—nodes, clusters, or organisms—preferentially along resonance corridors.

- **Appateurs \(A(x)\):**  
  **Label:** appateur rendering  
  Rendered as interface glyphs or portals; activation corresponds to visible “links” or jumps between distant regions.

---

## 6. Terminal serenity rendering

- **Label:** terminal serenity motif  
- In the terminal attractor:
  \[
  \mathcal{R} \to 0,\quad \mathcal{P} \to 0,\quad \lambda \to \lambda_{\max},\quad \sigma \to \sigma_{\max},
  \]
  the rendering collapses to:

  - flat, calm geometry  
  - minimal distortion  
  - no paradox warping  
  - stable, uniform color and topology  

A visual representation of NDH’s “final calm state.”
