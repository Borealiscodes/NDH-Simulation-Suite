# NDH Multi‑Domain Co‑Simulation Spec v2.4
A governed co‑simulation architecture enabling multiple NDH domains to run
simultaneously, exchange bounded signals, and maintain lineage‑anchored
stability across ecological, engineering, narrative, planetary, and governance
domains.

---

## 1. Purpose
v2.4 defines the runtime system for multi‑domain co‑simulation. It enables
multiple NDH domains to operate in parallel, interact through governed
coupling operators, and maintain stability through drift, resonance, and
collapse‑front containment rules.

This spec completes the NDH v2.x co‑simulation phase.

---

## 2. Co‑Simulation Architecture Overview
The multi‑domain co‑simulation consists of six layers:

1. **Domain Runtime Layer**  
2. **Coupling Operator Layer**  
3. **Transport Layer**  
4. **Stability Layer**  
5. **Governance Layer**  
6. **Visualization Layer**  

Each layer enforces strict boundaries to prevent domain bleed, collapse, or
unbounded resonance amplification.

---

## 3. Domain Runtime Layer
Each NDH domain runs as an isolated simulation unit.

### 3.1 Allowed Domain Types
- ecological domains  
- engineering domains  
- narrative domains  
- planetary domains  
- governance domains  

### 3.2 Domain Isolation Rules
- domains cannot modify each other’s internal state  
- domains cannot inject unbounded resonance  
- domains cannot override governance constraints  

---

## 4. Coupling Operator Layer
Defines how domains exchange signals.

### 4.1 Coupling Operators
- **O₁ (Drift Coupling)** — drift vectors  
- **O₂ (Resonance Coupling)** — resonance amplitudes  
- **O₃ (Collapse Coupling)** — collapse‑front flags  
- **O₄ (Recovery Coupling)** — recovery‑corridor activations  
- **O₅ (Governance Coupling)** — safety constraints  

### 4.2 Coupling Rules
- coupling must be **bounded**  
- coupling must be **bidirectional**  
- coupling must be **lineage‑anchored**  
- coupling must be **non‑coercive**  

### 4.3 Forbidden Couplings
- unilateral influence  
- collapse‑inducing resonance transfer  
- emotional bleed across domains  

---

## 5. Transport Layer
Defines how drift and resonance move between domains.

### 5.1 Drift Transport
- drift velocity capped per domain  
- drift direction must remain domain‑aligned  
- drift cannot exceed stability envelope  

### 5.2 Resonance Transport
- resonance amplitude dampened across domains  
- resonance cannot amplify during transport  
- resonance cannot cross governance boundaries  

### 5.3 Collapse‑Front Transport
Collapse fronts may propagate only if:
- ecological dampening active  
- engineering throttling active  
- governance override available  

---

## 6. Stability Layer
Ensures cross‑domain safety.

### 6.1 Collapse‑Front Containment
Collapse fronts are contained by:
- ecological dampening  
- engineering throttling  
- narrative buffering  
- governance override  

### 6.2 Recovery Corridor Activation
Recovery corridors activate when:
- drift velocity decreases  
- resonance amplitude dampens  
- engineering pressure drops  
- governance layer stabilizes  

### 6.3 Safe Mode
If containment fails:
- static diagrams only  
- capped resonance envelopes  
- lineage‑anchored summaries  

---

### 7.1 Governance Constraints
- emotional safety  
- climate responsibility

- non‑coercive pedagogy  
- narrative coherence  

### 7.2 Governance Overrides

- resonance exceeds limits  
- drift exceeds limits  
- collapse fronts propagate  
- domain bleed detected
