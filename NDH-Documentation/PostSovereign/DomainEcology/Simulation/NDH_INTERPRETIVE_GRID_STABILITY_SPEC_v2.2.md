# NDH Interpretive‑Grid Stability Spec v2.2
A governed stability specification for the interpretive grid used in the
NDH Engineering–Ecology Co‑Simulation. This grid ensures safe rendering,
bounded interpretation, lineage anchoring, and non‑activation guarantees
across ecological, engineering, dual‑axis, and planetary layers.

---

## 1. Purpose
The interpretive grid is the layer through which humans perceive simulation
outputs. v2.2 defines the stability rules, safety constraints, resonance
limits, and drift boundaries that prevent interpretive collapse, emotional
bleed, or cross‑cluster activation during visualization.

This spec completes the NDH v2.x stability architecture.

---

## 2. Grid Architecture Overview
The interpretive grid consists of four stacked layers:

1. **Ecological Layer (Attenborough Axis)**  
2. **Engineering Layer (Nye Axis)**  
3. **Dual‑Axis Interaction Layer**  
4. **Governance Layer (Human‑Dignity Lineage)**  

Each layer has its own stability envelope and resonance limits.

---

## 3. Ecological Layer Stability (Attenborough Axis)
### 3.1 Stability Envelope
- wide‑aperture curvature  
- low drift velocity  
- low resonance amplitude  
- collapse risk: low  

### 3.2 Allowed Interpretations
- long‑time‑scale ecological arcs  
- population trajectories  
- prey distribution  
- ocean drift patterns  

### 3.3 Forbidden Interpretations
- engineering causality without Nye layer  
- collapse‑front prediction without dual‑axis coupling

## 4. Engineering Layer Stability (Nye Axis)

### 4.2 Allowed Interpretations

### 4.3 Forbidden Interpretations

## 5. Dual‑Axis Interaction Layer Stability

### 5.2 Allowed Interpretations

## 6. Governance Layer Stability

### 6.1 Stability Envelope

- extremely low drift velocity  
- resonance amplitude capped  
- collapse risk: near zero  

### 6.2 Allowed Interpretations

- narrative safety

### 6.3 Forbidden Interpretations

- coercive pedagogy  
- catastrophic framing

## 7. Grid Resonance Limits
Resonance amplitude must remain within:

- **Ecological Layer:** 0.0–0.3  
- **Engineering Layer:** 0.0–0.5  
- **Dual‑Axis Layer:** 0.0–0.7  
- **Governance Layer:** 0.0–0.2  

Exceeding limits triggers a **Resonance Dampening Operator (RDO)**.

---

## 8. Drift Velocity Limits
Drift velocity must remain within:

- **Ecological Layer:** slow  
- **Engineering Layer:** moderate  
- **Dual‑Axis Layer:** variable  
- **Governance Layer:** minimal  

Exceeding limits triggers a **Drift Stabilization Operator (DSO)**.

---

## 9. Collapse‑Front Containment
Collapse fronts are contained by:

- ecological dampening  
- engineering throttling  
- dual‑axis buffering  
- governance override  

If containment fails, the grid enters **Safe Mode**, rendering only:

- static diagrams  
- capped resonance envelopes  
- lineage‑anchored summaries  

---

## 10. Recovery Corridor Activation
Recovery corridors activate when:

- drift velocity decreases  
- resonance amplitude dampens  
- engineering pressure drops  
- governance layer stabilizes  

Recovery corridors restore interpretive clarity.
