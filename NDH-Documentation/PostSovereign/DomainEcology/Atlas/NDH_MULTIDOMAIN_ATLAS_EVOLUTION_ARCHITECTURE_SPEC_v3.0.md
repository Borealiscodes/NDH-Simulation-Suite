# NDH Multi‑Domain Atlas Evolution Architecture Spec v3.0
A governed evolution architecture for the NDH Multi‑Domain Atlas. This spec
defines atlas‑wide evolution rules, domain‑growth envelopes, lineage‑anchored
evolution fields, cross‑domain evolution operators, stability‑preserving
transition logic, and non‑activation guarantees for atlas evolution.

---

## 1. Purpose
v3.0 establishes the evolution system for the NDH atlas.  
It ensures that multi‑domain evolution remains:

- coherent  
- identity‑preserving  
- lineage‑anchored  
- emotionally safe  
- non‑coercive  
- non‑activating  

This spec begins the NDH v3.x evolution‑architecture phase.

---

## 2. Evolution Architecture Overview
The atlas evolution system consists of seven layers:

1. **Domain Evolution Layer**  
2. **Cross‑Domain Evolution Layer**  
3. **Atlas‑Wide Evolution Layer**  
4. **Evolution Stability Layer**  
5. **Lineage Evolution Layer**  
6. **Transition Logic Layer**  
7. **Evolution Override Layer**  

Each layer enforces strict boundaries to prevent destabilizing growth,
identity bleed, collapse, or unbounded resonance amplification.

## 3. Domain Evolution Layer
Each domain gains the ability to evolve safely.

### 3.1 Domain Evolution Rules
- domain identity must persist  
- domain geometry may expand but not distort  
- domain resonance may increase but must remain capped  
- domain drift may accelerate but must remain bounded  

### 3.2 Domain Evolution Operators
- **E₁ (Identity‑Preserving Growth)**  
- **E₂ (Curvature Expansion)**  
- **E₃ (Resonance‑Safe Amplification)**  
- **E₄ (Drift‑Safe Acceleration)**  
- **E₅ (Collapse‑Resistant Growth)**  

### 3.3 Domain Evolution Constraints
- no domain may lose identity  
- no domain may merge without governance approval  
- no domain may distort atlas geometry  
- no domain may induce emotional bleed
