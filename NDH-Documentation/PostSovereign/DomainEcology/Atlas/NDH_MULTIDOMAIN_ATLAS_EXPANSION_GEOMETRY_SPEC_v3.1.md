## 2. Expansion Geometry Architecture Overview
The atlas expansion system consists of seven geometric layers:

1. **Domain Geometry Expansion Layer**  
2. **Cross‑Domain Geometry Expansion Layer**  
3. **Atlas‑Wide Geometry Expansion Layer**  
4. **Curvature Expansion Layer**  
5. **Drift‑Corridor Expansion Layer**

Each layer enforces strict boundaries to prevent destabilizing geometric
growth, identity bleed, collapse, or unbounded resonance amplification.

- domain topology may grow but must remain lineage‑anchored  
- domain drift corridors may extend but must remain stable  
- domain resonance chambers may enlarge but must remain capped  

### 3.2 Domain Geometry Expansion Operators
- **G₁ (Curvature Expansion Operator)**  
- **G₂ (Topology Growth Operator)**  
- **G₃ (Drift‑Corridor Extension Operator)**  
- **G₄ (Resonance‑Chamber Expansion Operator)**  
- **G₅ (Collapse‑Resistant Geometry Operator)**  

### 3.3 Domain Geometry Expansion Constraints
- no domain may distort atlas geometry  
- no domain may induce emotional bleed  
- no domain may merge geometry without governance approval  
- no domain may generate unbounded curvature
