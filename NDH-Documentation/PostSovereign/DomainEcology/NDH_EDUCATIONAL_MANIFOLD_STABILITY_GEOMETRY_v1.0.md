# NDH Educational Manifold — Stability Geometry v1.0
A geometric stability specification for the Attenborough–Nye Educational
Manifold, defining curvature fields, drift vectors, resonance envelopes,
collapse fronts, and emotional posture parity.

---

## 1. Purpose
This document defines the stability geometry of the Attenborough–Nye
Educational Manifold. It establishes the geometric structures that regulate
learner emotional load, cognitive drift, curiosity resonance, and collapse
risk. This layer is required before algebra, runtime, or governance can be
safely integrated.

---

## 2. Geometric Overview
The manifold consists of two primary curvature fields:

### Attenborough Curvature Field (Planetary Calm)
- low-frequency curvature
- wide-aperture semantic space
- stabilizing emotional geometry
- long-time-scale narrative arcs
- low drift velocity, high stability

### Nye Curvature Field (Engineering Energetic)
- high-frequency curvature
- narrow-aperture semantic space
- energizing cognitive geometry
- short-time-scale problem arcs
- high drift velocity, moderate stability

The manifold is non-dual: both fields coexist and interact without binary
collapse.

## 3. Drift Fields
Drift fields define how curiosity moves through the manifold.

### 3.1 Attenborough Drift
- slow, stable drift
- narrative-led movement
- low cognitive load
- ideal for P0–P1 emotional posture

### 3.2 Nye Drift
- fast, energetic drift
- engineering-led movement
- moderate cognitive load
- ideal for P1–P2 emotional posture

### 3.3 Cross-Axis Drift
Transport corridors allow drift between axes:
- planetary context → engineering solutions

## 4. Resonance Envelopes
Resonance envelopes define how emotional and cognitive excitation behaves.

### 4.1 Attenborough Resonance
- awe-based resonance
- calming oscillations
- low amplitude, high coherence
- reduces collapse risk

### 4.2 Nye Resonance
- excitement-based resonance
- high amplitude, high momentum
- increases drift velocity
- requires stability management

### 4.3 Resonance Interaction
Attenborough resonance dampens Nye resonance, preventing runaway excitation.

## 5. Collapse Fronts
Collapse fronts represent emotional or cognitive overload.

### Collapse Conditions
- excessive Nye resonance
- rapid cross-axis drift
- narrative fragmentation
- emotional posture P3

### Collapse Mitigation
- Attenborough calm-collapse operator
- stabilization corridors
- runtime interrupt handler

Collapse fronts are geometric indicators requiring runtime intervention.
