# **Ethical Partnership Roadmap (NDH + FO3)**  
### *A rights‑aligned development plan for atrocity‑documentation architecture using the NDH 50‑Dimensional Manifold*

---

## **I. Foundational Principles**  
- Courts determine liability **only when acting in good faith**; NDH must assume this is not guaranteed.  
- NDH reconstructs **patterns**, not guilt.  
- FO3 Law Arc governs ethical constraints and civil‑liberty safeguards.  
- Survivor dignity overrides institutional convenience.  
- Provenance integrity is mandatory.  
- NDH outputs must remain neutral, rights‑aligned, and non‑accusatory.

---

# **II. Phase 1 — Small‑Scale Testing**  
Begin with single‑event, well‑documented atrocities where judicial findings already exist.  
This ensures ethical stability and avoids premature entanglement with contested conflicts.

### **Recommended Test Cases**  
- **Srebrenica 1995**  
- **Rwanda 1994**  
- **Khmer Rouge**  
- **Yazidi Genocide**  
- **Rohingya 2017**  

### **Outputs**  
- 50D manifold prototype  
- Fragment‑to‑dimension mapping  
- Bias‑curvature correction validation  
- FO3 compliance geometry test  
- Preliminary **pattern‑of‑harm maps**

---

# **III. Phase 2 — Mid‑Scale Multi‑Event Reconstruction**  
Expand to multi‑incident clusters within a single conflict.

### **Examples**  
- Bosnia camp system  
- Argentina’s “Dirty War” disappearances  
- Sri Lanka end‑phase events  
- Syria chemical‑weapon incidents  

### **Outputs**  
- Cross‑event manifold stitching  
- Actor‑network mapping  
- Temporal‑spatial overlays  
- Multi‑source provenance mesh  
- FO3 ethical checkpoint

---

# **IV. Phase 3 — Judicial Good‑Faith Safeguard Layer**  
NDH must explicitly account for courts that:

- act in bad faith  
- cannot act in good faith  
- face political pressure  
- lack access to evidence  
- operate under asymmetrical power structures  

### **Safeguard Components**  
- **Judicial Good‑Faith Framework**  
- FO3 compliance geometry for judicial interfaces  
- Provenance mesh to prevent evidence distortion  
- Bias‑curvature correction to neutralize political narratives  
- Civil‑liberty firewall to prevent misuse of NDH outputs  

### **Purpose**  
NDH does not replace courts — it creates the **conditions** under which courts *can* act in good faith.

---

# **V. Phase 4 — Institutional Partnership Outreach**  
Partnerships should focus on archives, academic institutions, and technical bodies with strong ethical mandates.

### **Potential Partners**  
- Arolsen Archives  
- USHMM  
- Yad Vashem  
- UNOSAT  
- International Center for Transitional Justice (ICTJ)  
- University‑based genocide studies programs  
- National archives with atrocity documentation  
- Forensic anthropology labs  
- Digital preservation institutes  

### **Partnership Goals**  
- Ethical oversight  
- Access to curated archives  
- Survivor‑narrative protection  
- Methodological review  
- FO3 compliance auditing  
- Trauma‑informed design guidance

---

# **VI. Phase 5 — Hyperatlas Development**  
Only after all prior phases are stable.

### **Prerequisites**  
- Stable manifold  
- Validated bias‑correction geometry  
- Ethical oversight  
- Institutional partnership  
- FO3 governance spine fully implemented  
- Trauma‑informed design review  

### **Purpose**  
A comparative, rights‑aligned, multi‑atrocity analytical atlas — never a substitute for survivor testimony or judicial process.
