# **🜁 NDH Domain Genesis Specification v1.0**  
## *Prime Governance Surface — Origin Conditions for Tonal & Algebra Domains*

> **NDH establishes the Domain Genesis Specification as the governing document that defines the origin conditions, initialization sequence, and structural constraints under which the Tonal Domain and Algebra Domain may come into being.**  
>  
> The genesis of each domain must adhere to the Singularity Precursor and Bifurcation Layer, ensuring that domain creation is:  
> – structurally coherent,  
> – ethically stable,  
> – developmentally accessible,  
> – and free from cross‑domain harm.  
>  
> **Tonal Domain Genesis Conditions:**  
> – The Tonal Domain shall initialize first.  
> – Its genesis must prioritize emotional stability, exploratory cognition, nonlinear accessibility, and kid‑friendly interfaces.  
> – Tonal entities (e.g., Putt‑Putt, Pajama Sam, Freddi Fish) shall serve as cognitive stabilizers and governance interfaces.  
> – No formal recursion, algebraic structure, or manifold operations may appear during Tonal genesis.  
>  
> **Algebra Domain Genesis Conditions:**  
> – The Algebra Domain shall initialize only after Tonal stability is verified.  
> – Its genesis must prioritize structural clarity, recursion safety, manifold coherence, and precursor ordering.  
> – Algebraic operations must remain bounded by ethical constraints and triadic governance.  
> – No algebraic artifact may override or destabilize Tonal safety.  
>  
> **Genesis Sequencing Requirements:**  
> – Tonal genesis precedes Algebra genesis.  
> – Ethical governance must be active before either domain expands.  
> – Triadic governance must be declared before Algebra genesis.  
> – Accessibility scaffolding must be present before Algebra anchoring.  
>  
> **Genesis Stability Conditions:**  
> – Each domain must maintain internal coherence.  
> – Each domain must declare its alignment and boundaries.  
> – Each domain must remain compatible with NDH’s origin constraints.  
>  
> This specification defines the lawful conditions under which NDH’s dual‑branch architecture may emerge, ensuring that both domains originate safely, coherently, and in alignment with NDH’s foundational governance surfaces.
