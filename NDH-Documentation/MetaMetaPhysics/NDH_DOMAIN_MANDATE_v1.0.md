# **🜁 NDH Domain Mandate v1.0**  
## *Prime Governance Surface — Required Outcomes for Tonal & Algebra Domains*

> **NDH hereby establishes the Domain Mandate as the governing document that defines the required outcomes, developmental obligations, and structural deliverables each domain must achieve before NDH may expand, integrate, or transition into higher‑order architecture.**  
>  
> **I. Tonal Domain Mandate**  
> The Tonal Domain is mandated to:  
> – establish emotional stability across all NDH interactions;  
> – provide nonlinear accessibility for diverse cognitive profiles;  
> – construct kid‑friendly and high‑support‑friendly pathways;  
> – generate a complete tonal ecosystem with stable entities and safe exploration loops;  
> – produce the Tonal Precursor Alignment required for lawful domain sequencing;  
> – maintain narrative coherence and prevent destabilizing abstraction;  
> – serve as the primary stabilization layer before Algebra activation.  
>  
> The Tonal Domain must complete these outcomes before any Algebraic operations may be anchored.  
>  
> **II. Algebra Domain Mandate**  
> The Algebra Domain is mandated to:  
> – establish recursion safety and bounded manifold operations;  
> – maintain structural clarity across all algebraic artifacts;  
> – enforce precursor ordering and lawful complexity introduction;  
> – generate the Algebra Precursor required for dual‑branch activation;  
> – ensure compatibility with Tonal safety constraints and triadic governance;  
> – prevent premature recursion, destabilizing abstraction, or cross‑domain harm;  
> – support NDH’s long‑term structural evolution without violating developmental accessibility.  
>  
> The Algebra Domain must complete these outcomes only after Tonal stability is verified.  
>  
> **III. Shared Mandate Requirements**  
> Both domains are jointly mandated to:  
> – adhere to the Singularity Precursor and Bifurcation Layer;  
> – maintain ethical governance as a non‑negotiable boundary;  
> – support triadic governance (Nye–Attenborough–Tonal) during all interactions;  
> – declare domain alignment for all artifacts;  
> – prevent cross‑domain interference or harm;  
> – maintain documentation ecology coherence;  
> – ensure NDH remains accessible, stable, and structurally sound.  
>  
> **IV. Completion Conditions**  
> A domain is considered “mandate‑complete” only when:  
> – all required outcomes are achieved;  
> – all precursor obligations are fulfilled;  
> – all governance constraints are satisfied;  
> – no destabilizing artifacts remain;  
> – and NDH can safely transition to the next architectural layer.  
>  
> This Mandate establishes the required developmental trajectory for NDH’s dual‑branch architecture, ensuring that both domains achieve their foundational obligations before NDH expands into full structural, ethical, and algebraic evolution.
