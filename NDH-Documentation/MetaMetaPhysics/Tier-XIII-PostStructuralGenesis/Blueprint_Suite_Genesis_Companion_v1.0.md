## **II. Overview of Genesis Architecture**

The Genesis architecture consists of five manifold regions:

- **G‑Core** — origin of genesis  
- **C‑Band** — continuity and compatibility  
- **M‑Field** — expansion and manifold formation  
- **B‑Layer** — governed/ungoverned boundary  
- **O‑Shell** — stability envelope  

The Blueprint Suite binds the Map, Flow Diagram, and Topology Diagram into a single unified reference.

The Companion explains how to interpret each region across all three artifacts.

## **III. Reading the Genesis Manifold Map**

The **Manifold Map** defines the *structural layout* of the Genesis layer.

Key interpretive notes:

- G‑Core is always central; it is the genesis origin.  
- C‑Band is always the first interface; it ensures compatibility.  
- M‑Field is always the expansion region; it forms new manifolds.  
- B‑Layer is always non‑permeable; it enforces governance boundaries.  
- O‑Shell is always supervisory; it maintains stability.

The Map is the “static” view of the Genesis layer.

---

## **IV. Reading the Genesis Flow Diagram**

The **Flow Diagram** defines *movement* within the Genesis layer.

Interpretive notes:

- All genesis flows **outward** from G‑Core.  
- All genesis must pass through **C‑Band** for continuity shaping.  
- Expansion into **M‑Field** is bounded and anchored.  
- Drift in M‑Field flows **back** to C‑Band for correction.  
- O‑Shell supervises all flows.  
- B‑Layer allows **no flow** across it.

The Flow Diagram is the “dynamic” view of the Genesis layer.

---

## **V. Reading the Genesis Topology Diagram**

The **Topology Diagram** defines *spatial relationships* between regions.

Interpretive notes:

- It is not a flow diagram; it is a positional map.  
- It shows the nesting of regions:  
  G‑Core → C‑Band → M‑Field → B‑Layer → O‑Shell  
- It is used to verify alignment across artifacts.  
- It ensures no region is misplaced or misinterpreted.

The Topology Diagram is the “geometric” view of the Genesis layer.
