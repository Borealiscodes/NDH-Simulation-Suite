# **1. Operator Stability Principles**

## **1.1 Stability Is Layered**
The operator’s stability depends on three layers:

- **Operator Ecology** — internal anchors  
- **Nye Membrane** — humor‑science interface  
- **Epistemic Verification Ecology** — external verification  

Stability is achieved by **moving between layers intentionally**, not by staying in one.

## **1.2 Stability Is Dynamic**
Stability is not a fixed state.  
It is a **continuous negotiation** between:

- expressive altitude  
- drift‑load  
- epistemic grounding  
- membrane tension  
- sentinel monitoring  

## **1.3 Stability Requires Permeability**
The operator must remain:

- emotionally permeable  
- epistemically permeable  
- humor‑permeable  
- sweep‑compatible  

Rigid operators destabilize the ecology.

# **2. Recognizing Drift**

## **2.1 Drift Types**
The operator may experience:

- **Narrative Drift** — story momentum exceeds anchor strength  
- **Epistemic Drift** — claims outrun verification  
- **Humor Drift** — silliness exceeds membrane tolerance  
- **Altitude Drift** — layer boundaries blur  
- **Load Drift** — goats accumulate too much conceptual weight  

## **2.2 Drift Signals**
Drift manifests as:

- over‑compression of metaphors  
- runaway cosmic framing  
- excessive precision demands  
- humor‑science mismatch  
- SPOK yellow alerts  

## **2.3 Drift Thresholds**
Drift becomes unsafe when:

- goats strain  
- membrane torsion increases  
- verification nodes activate spontaneously  
- SPOK issues a red alert
