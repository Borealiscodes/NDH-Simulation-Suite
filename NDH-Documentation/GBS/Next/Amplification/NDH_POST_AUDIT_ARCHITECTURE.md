### NDH Post‑Audit Architecture

You’ve just certified the whole stack. Post‑audit, NDH needs an architecture that assumes:

- the Codices are stable  
- the boundary is safe  
- the substrate is healthy  
- the audits are complete  

This layer is the **operational regime** that runs *after* all that checking.

---

### 1. What NDH Post‑Audit Architecture is

**Label:** Definition  
NDH Post‑Audit Architecture is the **governed runtime geometry** that activates once:

- all four audits (sovereign, post‑sovereign, transition, architecture)  
- and the 50D Audit Ledger  

are complete and stable. It defines:

- how manifolds are allowed to move  
- how Codices are allowed to interact  
- how generativity is allowed to run  
- how transitions are scheduled and constrained  

It’s the “live physics” layer under certified conditions.

### 2. Core layers in the Post‑Audit Architecture

**Label:** Certified Sovereign Layer  
- Sovereign manifolds that passed the audit  
- Only permitted dissolution under governed triggers  

**Label:** Certified Substrate Layer  
- Post‑sovereign entities and domains  
- Generativity bounded by stability envelopes  

**Label:** Certified Boundary Layer  
- Transition corridors and dissolution bands  
- Only open when ledger conditions are met  

**Label:** Governance & Scheduling Layer  
- Rules for when transitions can occur  
- Rate limits, envelope checks, narrative pressure thresholds  

**Label:** Ledger‑Coupled Control Layer  
- Every operation references the 50D Audit Ledger  
- No transition or activation without dimensional clearance

**Label:** Audits → Runtime Constraints  
- Sovereign audit defines allowed dissolution patterns

- Transition audit defines allowed corridor usage  
- Architecture audit defines allowed global configurations  

**Label:** Ledger → Global Policy  
- 50D ledger becomes the **policy spine**  
- Dimensional thresholds become runtime guardrails
