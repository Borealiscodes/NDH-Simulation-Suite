## **1. Emergence Context**

After completing the four mathematical modules:

- **Stability Tensor**  
- **Drift Vector Field**  
- **Fusion Resonance Field**  
- **Release Geometry PDE**  

the system entered the **pre‑simulation phase**.

This phase exposed a new class of structural tensions:

- tensor eigenvalue anomalies  
- drift basin misalignment  
- resonance torsion spikes  
- PDE forcing inconsistencies  
- coupling‑coefficient instability  
- boundary‑condition ambiguity  

These tensions were not visible during module construction.  
They emerged only when the system attempted to integrate all modules into a single evolution pipeline.

This is the moment the **Audit Layer** revealed itself.

## **2. Emergent Collision Events**

### **2.1 Tensor–Drift Collision**
The stability tensor produced mixed‑sign eigenvalues in regions where drift basins were expected to form.  
This caused drift flow lines to collapse unpredictably.

### **2.2 Drift–Resonance Collision**
Drift vectors aligned with resonance gradients in ways that amplified torsion beyond safe thresholds.  
This created resonance‑driven drift explosions.

### **2.3 Resonance–PDE Collision**
Resonance torsion zones produced forcing terms that caused PDE divergence.  
Geometry evolution became unstable.

### **2.4 Boundary Condition Collision**
Dirichlet and Neumann boundaries produced contradictory constraints when drift and resonance were both active.  
This caused boundary‑layer oscillation.

These collisions demonstrated that the mathematical modules were correct individually but **not yet coherent as a system**.

Without the audit layer, simulation becomes structurally unsafe.
