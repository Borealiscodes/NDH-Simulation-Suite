### Museum Sensory Palette + Tour‑Type Crossmap (NDH Anchor v1.0)

Here’s the clean, final version of the **Museum Sensory Palette Anchor** with its **Tour‑Type Crossmap**, ready to sit after RNIB in the build order.

---

#### 1. Anchor identity

**Label:** Museum Sensory Palette Anchor  
**Role:** Second NDH structural anchor (positive manifold)  
**Scope:** Multi‑modal museum environments, guided tours, exhibit traversal, spatial storytelling.

This anchor defines how NDH understands **rich, multi‑modal spaces** instead of sensory‑void states.

---

#### 2. Sensory palette (positive manifold)

**Visual (V):**  
- **High:** exhibits, signage, spatial cues, lighting gradients.  

**Audio (A):**  
- **Layered:** ambient sound, narration, audio guides, localized soundscapes.  

**Tactile (T):**  
- **Interactive:** touchable artifacts, textured surfaces, haptic interfaces.  

**Smell (O):**  
- **Contextual:** themed rooms (e.g., historical, industrial, natural).  

**Taste (S):**  
- **Peripheral:** café, food exhibits, occasional tasting experiences.  

**Spatial (P):**  
- **Structured:** rooms, corridors, wings, vertical levels, wayfinding.

NDH maps this to a **full positive manifold**:

\[
\mathcal{P}(x) = \{V, A, T, O, S, P\}
\]

---

#### 3. Tour‑type geometry

**Guided Tour:**  
- **Label:** High narrative control  
- **Effect:** Strong pacing, curated route, fixed modality sequence.  

**Self‑Guided Tour:**  
- **Label:** High user agency  
- **Effect:** Variable pacing, branching paths, modality choice.  

**Thematic Tour:**  
- **Label:** Concept‑driven  
- **Effect:** Cross‑room narrative arcs, motif‑based sensory emphasis.  

**Accessibility‑Focused Tour:**  
- **Label:** Modality‑optimized  
- **Effect:** Reduced cognitive load, enhanced fallback channels, clear wayfinding.

NDH treats tour types as **routing geometries** over \(\mathcal{P}(x)\).

---

#### 4. Crossmap: Sensory palette × tour types

**Integrity routing:**  
- **Label:** Tour types must preserve sensory integrity  
- **Effect:** No route may rely on a single fragile modality (e.g., visual‑only).

**Curvature routing:**  
- **Label:** Emotional + cognitive pacing  
- **Effect:** Tours modulate intensity—quiet rooms, dense rooms, rest points.

**Fallback routing:**  
- **Label:** Alternate paths  
- **Effect:** Audio/tactile‑friendly routes, low‑vision‑friendly paths.

This crossmap defines how **museum space is traversed as a sensory graph**, not just a floorplan.

---

#### 5. NDH structural mapping

**Sensory palette manifold:**  
\[
\mathcal{P}(x) = \{V, A, T, O, S, P\}
\]

**Tour‑type routing function:**  
\[
\mathcal{T}(x) = \text{path over } \mathcal{P}(x)
\]

**Crossmap:**  
\[
\mathcal{M}_{Museum} = (\mathcal{P}, \mathcal{T})
\]

This becomes NDH’s **positive manifold anchor**, complementing RNIB’s **void manifold anchor**.
