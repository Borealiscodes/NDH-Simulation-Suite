### NDH Multi‑Manifold Support v2.0  
*Turning NDH from “a manifold” into “a universe.”*

---

## 1. Purpose  

NDH Multi‑Manifold Support v2.0 defines how **multiple NDH manifolds** run in parallel, interact safely, and share governance:

- multiple independent manifolds  
- inter‑manifold bridges (corridors)  
- cross‑manifold tensor influence  
- shared HRD/JDTR governance  
- Dark Mode v2.0 across manifolds  
- K‑space expansion across dimensions  

It is the **scaling layer** of NDH v2.0.

---

## 2. Core concepts  

- **Manifold:** a single NDH stability space (basins, corridors, margins, tensors).  
- **Manifold namespace:** unique ID + configuration for each manifold.  
- **Inter‑manifold bridge:** corridor connecting two manifolds.  
- **Cross‑manifold tensor influence:** controlled propagation of tensor effects.  
- **Shared governance:** HRD/JDTR across all manifolds.  

---

## 3. Multi‑manifold runtime behavior  

- multiple manifolds run concurrently under NDH Runtime Engine v2.0  
- each manifold has:  
  - its own geometry envelope  
  - its own tensor fields  
  - its own ladder modes  
  - its own accessibility profiles  
- HRD validates:  
  - per‑manifold safety  
  - cross‑manifold interactions  
  - Dark Mode visibility across manifolds  

---

## 4. Inter‑manifold bridges  

- bridges are **corridors** that connect manifolds:  
  - define entry/exit basins  
  - define allowed tensor influence  
  - define geometry continuity rules  
- HRD enforces:  
  - no unsafe tensor spikes across bridges  
  - no unstable margin crossings  
  - accessibility‑safe transitions  

---

## 5. Cross‑manifold tensor rules  

- tensors may influence other manifolds only via:  
  - defined bridges  
  - HRD‑approved influence rules  
- allowed patterns:  
  - soft resonance coupling  
  - limited curvature influence  
  - no direct fold propagation without HRD approval  

---

## 6. K‑space expansion  

- higher‑dimensional K‑space layers can span multiple manifolds  
- rules define:  
  - rank constraints  
  - stability envelopes  
  - Dark Mode dimensional inversion  
- JDTR logs all cross‑manifold K‑space events.

---

## 7. Accessibility and governance  

- accessibility profiles apply per manifold and across bridges  
- HRD governs:  
  - multi‑manifold safety  
  - Dark Mode cross‑visibility  
  - tensor influence legality  
- JDTR records:  
  - manifold interactions  
  - bridge usage  
  - cross‑manifold anomalies  

---

## 8. Extension rules  

Any multi‑manifold extension must include:

- manifold impact analysis  
- bridge design and safety rules  
- tensor influence constraints  
- accessibility compliance plan  
- HRD validation plan  
- JDTR logging schema  

No extension may bypass shared governance.
