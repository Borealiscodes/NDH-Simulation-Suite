# 🜁 **I. Real Museum Tour Structures (Actual Practice)**  
### *These are the standard formats used worldwide.*





- **Docent‑Led Tour** — A trained guide leads visitors through curated highlights.  
- **Self‑Guided Tour** — Visitors explore at their own pace using maps or audio guides.  
- **Thematic Tour** — Focus on one theme (e.g., “Ancient Egypt,” “Innovation”).  
- **Narrative Tour** — A story ties the exhibits together.  
- **Immersive Tour** — Interactive rooms, sensory experiences, AR/VR.  
- **Expert Deep Dive** — For advanced visitors; highly detailed.  
- **Accessibility Tour** — Designed for cognitive, sensory, or mobility accessibility.

These are the “adult museum” patterns.

---

# 🜂 **II. Kids’ Museum Tour Structures (Actual Practice)**  
### *These simplify complexity and maximize engagement.*





- **Character‑Led Tour** — A mascot or character guides the child.  
- **Quest Tour** — Kids solve puzzles or complete missions.  
- **Hands‑On Tour** — Touch, build, interact.  
- **Storybook Tour** — Exhibits become chapters in a story.  
- **Color‑Coded Pathways** — Kids follow colored lines on the floor.  
- **Emotion‑Based Tour** — “Find things that make you curious,” etc.  
- **Sensory Tour** — Lights, sounds, textures.

These are the “kid museum” patterns.
