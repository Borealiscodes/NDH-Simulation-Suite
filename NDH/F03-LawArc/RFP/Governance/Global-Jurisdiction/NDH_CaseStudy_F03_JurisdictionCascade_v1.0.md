## **I. Scenario Overview (Anonymized)**  
A single human‑rights defender (“Subject‑A”) attempts to seek redress across multiple jurisdictions.  
The system reacts with **procedural overload**, **bureaucratic recursion**, and **cross‑border failure cascades**.

This case is used to model:

- **High‑velocity filings vs. institutional reaction thresholds**  
- **Diplomatic channel exploitation of volume**  
- **Non‑linear escalation patterns**  
- **Systemic brittleness under multi‑vector human rights claims**  
- **Failure of “safe third country” assumptions under stress**

This is a **simulation‑only artifact** intended to test NDH’s governance‑architecture modules.

## **II. NDH Problem Frame**  
### **F03‑LawArc Stressor Class:**  
**“Multi‑Jurisdictional Human Rights Cascade with Procedural Suppression Feedback Loops.”**

### **Core Dynamics:**  
- **Volume‑based suppression:** Institutions respond to high‑frequency filings with defensive posture.

- **Cross‑border recursion:** Failures in one jurisdiction propagate into others.
