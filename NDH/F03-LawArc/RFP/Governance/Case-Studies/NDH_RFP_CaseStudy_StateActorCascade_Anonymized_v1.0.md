## **IV. Narrative (Anonymized)**

### **Phase 1 — Domestic Trigger Event**
Subject‑A reports systemic misconduct within a domestic agency.  
The agency retaliates through:

- Misuse of criminal charges  
- Prolonged detention beyond statutory limits  
- Medical neglect  
- Psychological coercion  
- Suppression of evidence

This creates the **initial instability vector**.

---

### **Phase 2 — Attempted Legal Redress**
Subject‑A attempts emergency filings in domestic courts.  
Clerks reject filings on procedural grounds despite:

- Clear statutory jurisdiction  
- Urgent human‑rights implications  
- Evidence of torture  
- Imminent risk of harm

This triggers the **bureaucratic cruelty manifold**.

---

### **Phase 3 — Cross‑Border Escalation**
Subject‑A flees to a second country and attempts asylum.  
Authorities respond with:

- Illegal detention  
- Misclassification as “serious criminality”  
- Forced withdrawal of asylum claim  
- Constructive refoulement  
- Diplomatic coordination with the original state

This forms the **transnational repression basin**.

---

### **Phase 4 — Multi‑Jurisdictional Filings**
Subject‑A files:

- Emergency injunctions  
- Human rights petitions  
- ICC referrals  
- UN Special Procedures submissions  
- Appeals to constitutional courts  
- Requests for diplomatic asylum

The **volume** and **speed** of filings create a **reactive suppression feedback loop**:

- Clerks refuse filings  
- Courts ignore submissions  
- NHRIs deny jurisdiction  
- Diplomatic missions exploit procedural gaps  
- Governments coordinate to avoid liability

---

### **Phase 5 — Diplomatic Pressure Exploitation**
Multiple states begin using:

- “Informal” diplomatic channels  
- Silence  
- Delay  
- Non‑response  
- Procedural obstruction  
- Threats of refoulement  
- Misgendering and deadnaming  
- Disability‑based discrimination

This is the **Diplomatic Liability Avoidance Basin** in NDH terms.

---

### **Phase 6 — Systemic Collapse of Access to Remedy**
Across jurisdictions:

- No court accepts filings  
- No NHRI processes complaints  
- No asylum system functions lawfully  
- No diplomatic mission provides protection  
- No international body intervenes

This is the **full collapse of the Human Rights Integrity Axis**.

## **VI. Strategic Insight for NDH Simulation**

### **Key Finding**
**High‑volume multi‑jurisdictional action increases the likelihood of coordinated suppression.**

### **Optimal Strategy (Derived from Case)**
- Limit actions to **one strong action per day**  
- Build **momentum**, not **landslides**  
- Avoid triggering bureaucratic panic  
- Maintain **predictable cadence**  
- Use **precision filings**, not volume  
- Exploit **single‑jurisdiction stability pockets**  
- Avoid simultaneous diplomatic pressure vectors

This becomes a **governance‑layer heuristic** inside NDH.
