# 🌌 **Stability Envelope Specification v1.0**  
### *Formal NDH Definition of Stability Envelopes, Basin Geometry, and Tensor Alignment*

---

## ⭐ **1. Purpose of Stability Envelopes**

A **stability envelope** is the NDH construct that defines the safe region of operation for a cognitive, experiential, or system manifold. It ensures:

- identity preservation  
- collapse avoidance  
- HRD‑safe behavior  
- tensor alignment  
- basin continuity across K‑space  

It is the geometric structure that prevents ego metrics, external valuation, and self‑judgment from inducing curvature spikes, stress overload, or fold events.

---

## ⭐ **2. Formal Definition**

A stability envelope \( \mathcal{E} \) is defined as:

\[
\mathcal{E}(x) = \{ x \mid G_{ij}(x) \geq \tau_{\text{HRD}} \}
\]

Where:

- \( G_{ij}(x) \) = **Geometry Tensor**  
- \( \tau_{\text{HRD}} \) = **HRD stability threshold**  
- \( x \) = point in the manifold  

This means:

> A point is inside the stability envelope if its geometry tensor satisfies HRD constraints.

- elevation

- K‑space layers  

This is the “rock at the base and rock at the peak” condition.

## ⭐ **4. Geometry Tensor Construction**

The geometry tensor is defined as:

\[
G_{ij}(x) = \alpha I_{ij}(x) - \beta C_{ij}(x) - \gamma S_{ij}(x)
\]

Where:

- \( \alpha \) = invariant weight  
- \( \beta \) = curvature penalty  
- \( \gamma \) = stress penalty  

This tensor determines:

- basin shape  
- envelope boundaries  
- chaos margins  
- redesign corridors

\[
\mathcal{B} = \{ x \mid G_{ij}(x) \text{ is locally maximal} \}
\]

Properties:

- low curvature

- high invariants  
- stable oscillatory patterns  

Basins are the **home regions** of the stability envelope.
