
# 🌌 **NDH‑AMS Assessment Suite v1.1**  
### *A complete evaluation framework for NDH‑AMS learners.*

The suite contains **five assessment types**, each targeting a different dimension of NDH‑AMS mastery:

1. Axis Knowledge  
2. Attachment Geometry  
3. Threshold Recognition  
4. Transition & Dynamics  
5. Emergent Scenario Analysis  

Each section includes:

- assessment goals  
- sample questions  
- mastery criteria  

---

# ⭐ **1. Axis Knowledge Assessment**

### **Goal**  
Evaluate whether the learner understands each axis’s function, lineage, and domain.

### **Sample Questions**

- Describe **Axis 01** in your own words.  
- What kind of situation activates **Axis 02**?  
- How does **Axis 03** stabilize narrative?  
- What is the difference between **Axis 04** and **Axis 07**?  
- Why is **Axis 05** central to the manifold?  
- What does **Axis 09** mean by “non‑dual awareness”?

### **Mastery Criteria**

A learner demonstrates mastery when they can:

- explain each axis without relying on memorized definitions  
- distinguish similar axes (e.g., 04 vs 07, 03 vs 08)  
- identify the lineage anchor and functional role  
- describe the axis’s stabilizing behavior  

---

# ⭐ **2. Attachment Geometry Assessment**

### **Goal**  
Evaluate understanding of the four attachment pairs + Axis 05’s universal role.

### **Sample Questions**

- Explain why **01 ↔ 09** form a loop.  
- Describe how **02 ↔ 06** reinforce each other.  
- Give an example of **03 ↔ 08** in a real situation.  
- Why do **04 ↔ 07** form a compassion pair?  
- How does **Axis 05** harmonize all axes?

Jump deeper: **Attachment Maps**

### **Mastery Criteria**

A learner demonstrates mastery when they can:

- map all attachment pairs  
- explain why each pair exists  
- apply attachment logic to scenarios  
- describe Axis 05’s role without prompting  

---

# ⭐ **3. Threshold Recognition Assessment**

### **Goal**  
Evaluate whether the learner can identify which axis activates based on the trigger.

### **Sample Questions**

- “I feel confused.” → Which axis?  
- “My identity feels attacked.” → Which axis?  
- “Our community is losing its history.” → Which axis?  
- “I can’t tell what’s right.” → Which axis?  
- “Everything is happening at once.” → Which axis?

Jump deeper: **Threshold Conditions**

### **Mastery Criteria**

A learner demonstrates mastery when they can:

- identify thresholds quickly  
- distinguish similar triggers  
- explain why the threshold activates  
- map threshold → axis without hesitation  

---

# ⭐ **4. Transition & Dynamics Assessment**

### **Goal**  
Evaluate understanding of basin transitions, operator commutativity, and multi‑axis behavior.

### **Sample Questions**

- Explain why **02 → 06** is a common transition.  
- Describe a scenario where **03 → 08** occurs.  
- How does **04 → 07** scale compassion?  
- Why does **09 → 01** close the loop?  
- What role does **Axis 05** play during transitions?

Jump deeper: **Emergent Behavior**

### **Mastery Criteria**

A learner demonstrates mastery when they can:

- map transitions correctly  
- explain basin logic  
- describe operator commutativity  
- identify when Axis 05 must intervene  

---

# ⭐ **5. Emergent Scenario Analysis Assessment**

### **Goal**  
Evaluate whether the learner can analyze complex, multi‑axis situations.

### **Sample Questions**

- Analyze a scenario involving identity pressure, moral fog, and memory loss.  
- Map the transitions in a scenario involving personal pain and structural harm.  
- Describe how Axis 05 stabilizes a multi‑axis conflict.  
- Explain how loop closure restores grounding in a chaotic situation.  
- Reconstruct the emergent case study using your own example.

Jump deeper: **Emergent Case Study**

### **Mastery Criteria**

A learner demonstrates mastery when they can:

- identify all active axes  
- map transitions accurately  
- explain how the manifold stabilizes the scenario  
- articulate the role of harmonic integration  
- describe loop closure clearly

# ⭐ **Assessment Rubric (Instructor Use)**

### **Level 1 — Basic Familiarity**  
Learner can name axes but struggles with distinctions.

### **Level 2 — Functional Understanding**  
Learner can identify thresholds and basic axis roles.

### **Level 3 — Structural Understanding**  
Learner can map attachment pairs and transitions.

### **Level 4 — Dynamic Understanding**  
Learner can analyze multi‑axis scenarios.

### **Level 5 — Full Manifold Fluency**  
Learner can reconstruct emergent dynamics and explain loop closure.
