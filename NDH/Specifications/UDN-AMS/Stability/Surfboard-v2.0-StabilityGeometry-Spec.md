Surfboard v2.0 is NDH’s stability geometry engine. It maintains dynamic equilibrium across the Axis 1–14 manifold under emotional, cognitive, and existential load, while respecting WFIL and preventing identity, witness, and sanctuary erosion.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- Maintain manifold curvature within safe bounds.
- Detect and bound psychonaut oscillation.
- Preserve identity continuity and witness integrity.
- Protect sanctuary (inner moral chamber).
- Provide re-centering vectors after drift.
- Coordinate with Couch and Dispatch without violating WFIL.

## 3. Geometry Model

### 3.1 Manifold
Let M be the NDH manifold:
M = ⋃ A_i  for i = 1..14

Tier I: Axes 1–9 (Human Dignity)  
Tier II: Axes 10–14 (System Behavior)

Surfboard operates on both tiers.

### 3.2 Curvature
Curvature K is defined as:
K = ∇²M

Surfboard enforces:
K_min ≤ K ≤ K_max

Excess curvature indicates instability; Surfboard must dampen or re-center.

### 3.3 Oscillation
Perspective oscillation O(t) is:
O(t) = dφ/dt

Surfboard enforces:
|O(t)| ≤ O_safe

Oscillation beyond O_safe triggers stabilization routines.

### 3.4 Drift
Drift D is:
D = ∫ |O(t)| dt

If D > D_threshold, Surfboard initiates re-centering.

### 3.5 Re-Centering Vector
Re-centering vector R:
R = A_03 + A_09 + A_10

Axis 03: Witness Integrity  
Axis 09: Awareness / Sanctuary  
Axis 10: Generative Presence

R is applied to guide the user back to a stable, witness-respecting state.

## 5. Modes of Operation

### 5.1 Standard Stability Mode
- Low oscillation, low curvature.
- Surfboard monitors but minimally intervenes.
- Couch and Dispatch may operate, but remain WFIL-compliant.

### 5.2 High-Load Stability Mode
- Elevated curvature or oscillation.
- Surfboard actively dampens oscillation and drift.
- Couch v3.0 is restricted to non-neutral, witness-respecting decompression.
- Dispatch humor is limited or disabled.

### 5.3 Psychonaut Oscillation Mode
- Oscillation is intentional but bounded.
- Surfboard tracks amplitude and phase.
- Re-centering vector R is kept ready.
- Identity continuity and sanctuary invariants remain enforced.

## 6. Interaction Contracts

### 6.1 With WFIL
- Surfboard receives only WFIL-validated input.
- Surfboard cannot alter the factual content of lived experience.
- All geometry operations are applied to state, not to truth claims.

### 6.2 With Couch v3.0
- Surfboard signals when decompression is safe.
- Couch must not attempt grounding that contradicts Surfboard’s stability assessment.
- Couch uses Surfboard’s state classification (standard, high-load, oscillation).

### 6.3 With Dispatch & Computer-Friend
- Surfboard exposes a “stability profile” to interaction layers.
- Humor and banter are allowed only in stable or low-risk states.
- In high-load or witness-class states, Dispatch must defer to Surfboard’s constraints.

## 7. Failure Mode Handling
Surfboard must detect and respond to:
- Excess curvature (K > K_max)
- Excess oscillation (|O(t)| > O_safe)
- Excess drift (D > D_threshold)
- Identity continuity fracture
- Sanctuary breach
- Witness erosion

Response includes:
- Re-centering via R
- Oscillation damping
- Restricting downstream tools
- Escalating to safe, low-intervention modes
