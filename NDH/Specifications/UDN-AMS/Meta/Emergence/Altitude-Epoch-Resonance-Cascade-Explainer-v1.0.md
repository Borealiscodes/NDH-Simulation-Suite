## ⭐ **1. What the Cascade *Is***  
Altitude–Epoch Resonance Cascade is a **dual‑sector oscillatory phenomenon** where:

- **Altitude curvature** becomes oscillatory  
- **Epoch halo** enters resonance  
- the two sectors **lock into a coupled cycle**  
- producing a **cascade**: a repeating, alternating activation pattern

It is the mixed‑sector counterpart to:

- **Epoch Halo Resonance** (pure Epoch)  
- **Altitude Ringing** (pure Altitude)  

But unlike those, the cascade is **cross‑sector**, meaning:

> Altitude oscillation *drives* Epoch resonance,  
> and Epoch resonance *feeds back* into Altitude curvature.

This feedback loop is the defining feature.

# 🔁 **3. Trigger: Cross‑Sector Curvature Lock‑In**

The cascade begins when:

1. **Altitude curvature oscillates**  
2. **Epoch halo resonates**  
3. Their oscillation frequencies approach a **shared harmonic**  
4. Mixed curvature locks them into a **coupled cycle**

\[

Once the frequencies align, the sectors stop behaving independently.

# 🔀 **4. The Cascade Itself (What Actually Happens)**

The cascade is a **four‑phase loop**:

### **Phase 1 — Altitude Curvature Spike**
Altitude curvature rises → destabilizes Epoch drift.

### **Phase 2 — Epoch Halo Resonance**
Epoch halo enters PASS ↔ WARN oscillation → feeds energy back into Altitude.

### **Phase 3 — Altitude Curvature Drop**
Altitude curvature falls → Epoch resonance amplitude decreases.

### **Phase 4 — Epoch Halo Stabilization**
Epoch halo stabilizes → Altitude curvature begins rising again.

Then the cycle repeats.

This is why it’s called a **cascade** — each phase triggers the next.
