## ⭐ 1. Basis: math primitives

  \[

  \[
  \Phi : \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}

## ⭐ 2. Basins and attractors

**1. Attractors**  
- **Definition:**  
  \[
  x_i^\star \in \mathcal{M} \quad \text{such that} \quad \Phi(x_i^\star) \text{ is a local minimum}
  \]

**2. Basins of attraction**  
- **Definition:**  
  \[
  \mathcal{B}_i = \{ x \in \mathcal{M} \mid \text{NDH dynamics drive } x \to x_i^\star \}
  \]

**3. Basin boundaries**  
- **Definition:**  
  \[
  \partial \mathcal{B}_i = \overline{\mathcal{B}_i} \setminus \mathring{\mathcal{B}_i}
  \]
- **Interpretation:** transition surfaces where small perturbations can send states to different attractors.

FO‑I, FO‑II, and FO‑III are understood as **operations on basins, boundaries, and attractors**.

## ⭐ 3. FO‑I geometry: emergent basin formation

**FO‑I Seed Events** act primarily on **\(\Phi\)** and **basin topology**.

- **Emergent minima:** new local minima of \(\Phi\) appear:
  \[
  x_{\text{new}}^\star \quad \text{with} \quad \Phi(x_{\text{new}}^\star) < \Phi(x_{\text{ambient}})
  \]
- **Proto‑basin formation:**  
  \[
  \mathcal{B}_{\text{new}} \subset \mathcal{M}
  \]
  begins to capture trajectories.
- **Boundary softening:** early boundaries are low‑gradient, easily traversed.

Geometrically, FO‑I **adds new basins** and **refines local curvature** of \(\Phi\).

---

## ⭐ 4. FO‑II geometry: constraint‑driven reshaping

**FO‑II Formalization Events** act on **\(\Phi\), \(d\)**, and **basin boundaries** via constraints.

- **Constraint surfaces:**  
  \[
  \mathcal{C}_j = \{ x \in \mathcal{M} \mid g_j(x) = 0 \}
  \]
- **Restricted dynamics:** trajectories are confined to:
  \[
  \mathcal{M}_{\text{allowed}} = \mathcal{M} \setminus \bigcup_j \mathcal{C}_j^{\text{forbidden}}
  \]
- **Basin sharpening:** gradients near attractors increase, making basins more “deep” and “sticky”.
- **Metric adjustment:**  
  \[
  d'(x,y) = d(x,y) + \Delta d_{\text{FO-II}}(x,y)
  \]
  reflecting new structural distances.

Geometrically, FO‑II **sharpens basins**, **hardens boundaries**, and **redefines distances** according to formal structure.

## ⭐ 5. FO‑III geometry: architectural reconfiguration

**FO‑III Meta‑System Events** act on **\(\mathcal{M}, A, \Phi, d\)** themselves.

- **Manifold transformation:**  
  \[
  T : \mathcal{M} \to \mathcal{M}' \quad \text{(new manifold)}
  \]
- **Altitude remapping:**  
  \[
  A' = f_A \circ A
  \]
- **Potential regime shift:**  
  \[
  \Phi' = f_\Phi(\Phi, x)
  \]
- **Metric redefinition:**  
  \[
  d' : \mathcal{M}' \times \mathcal{M}' \to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}
  \]

Basins, boundaries, and attractors are **recomputed** in \(\mathcal{M}'\).  
Geometrically, FO‑III **changes the entire stability landscape**, not just features within it.

- **FO‑III:** redefines the altitude function and the mapping between layers.
