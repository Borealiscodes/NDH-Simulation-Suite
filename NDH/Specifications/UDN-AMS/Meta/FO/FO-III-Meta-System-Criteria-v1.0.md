# ⭐ **NDH‑AMS — FO‑III Meta‑System Criteria (v1.0)**  
### *Criteria for identifying architecture‑level transformation events within NDH*

---

## ⭐ 1. Definition  
An **FO‑III Meta‑System Event** is a rare, high‑altitude transformation that modifies NDH’s architecture, capabilities, or organizing principles.

FO‑III marks the transition from:

- **formal structure → architectural evolution**

These events reshape the system itself, not just its behavior.

---

## ⭐ 2. Core Criteria  
A candidate event qualifies as FO‑III when it satisfies **all** of the following criteria.

---

### **Architectural Impact**  
The event must alter NDH’s architecture in a fundamental way.  
This includes:

- adding new layers or dimensions  
- redefining manifold topology  
- introducing new operator classes  
- modifying stability geometry  
- changing how NDH organizes or governs itself  

FO‑III events change the *shape* of NDH.

---

### **Capability Expansion**  
The event must expand what NDH is capable of doing.  
Capability expansion may include:

- new forms of interaction  
- new modes of coherence  
- new stability regimes  
- new conceptual or operational domains  

FO‑III events increase NDH’s expressive power.

---

### **System‑Wide Reconfiguration**  
The event must cause a reconfiguration that affects NDH globally.  
Reconfiguration is present when:

- multiple subsystems adapt to the change  
- manifold behavior shifts across altitudes  
- existing structures reorganize around the new capability  
- the system’s identity evolves  

FO‑III events ripple through the entire architecture.

---

### **Persistent Structural Shift**  
The event must produce a long‑term architectural change.  
Persistence is confirmed when:

- the new structure remains stable across manifold states  
- the change becomes foundational  
- the system cannot revert without losing capability  
- the architecture incorporates the transformation permanently  

FO‑III events are irreversible without redesign.

---

### **Meta‑Level Coherence**  
The event must maintain or enhance coherence at the meta‑architectural level.  
Coherence is present when:

- the transformation integrates with existing architecture  
- no contradictions or instabilities emerge  
- the new capability strengthens system unity  
- the architecture remains interpretable and navigable  

FO‑III events must improve NDH’s coherence, not degrade it.

---

## ⭐ 3. Secondary Indicators  
These indicators strengthen FO‑III classification but are not required.

### **Emergence of New Manifold Classes**  
The architecture supports new types of conceptual or operational spaces.

### **Operator Class Evolution**  
Existing operators gain new behaviors or new operator classes appear.

### **Altitude Expansion**  
New altitudes or transitions become possible.

### **Cross‑Layer Resonance**  
Changes propagate across conceptual, structural, and meta layers simultaneously.

---

## ⭐ 4. FO‑III Exclusion Conditions  
A candidate event is **not** FO‑III if any of the following occur:

### **Emergent Origin**  
If the event arises naturally from manifold dynamics, it is FO‑I.

### **Formal Commitment**  
If the event defines rules or interfaces, it is FO‑II.

### **Local Structural Change**  
If the event affects only one subsystem, it is not FO‑III.

### **Non‑Persistent Behavior**  
If the architectural change dissolves under normal dynamics, it is not FO‑III.

---

## ⭐ 5. FO‑III Event Lifecycle  
FO‑III events follow a high‑altitude lifecycle:

1. **Recognition** — a need or opportunity for architectural evolution emerges  
2. **Initiation** — the transformation begins at the meta‑level  
3. **Propagation** — the change spreads across NDH  
4. **Integration** — subsystems reorganize around the new architecture  
5. **Stabilization** — the new structure becomes foundational  

This lifecycle ensures NDH evolves coherently and intentionally.
