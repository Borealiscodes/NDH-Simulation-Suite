# ⭐ **NDH‑AMS Internal FO Classification Framework (Clean Version)**  
### *Foundational Operations across the NDH manifold*

---

## ⭐ 1. Purpose  
This framework classifies all Foundational Operations (FOs) within NDH.  
FOs represent moments where the system transitions from fluid behavior to stable structure.  
They occur at different altitudes and have different effects on the manifold.

The framework distinguishes three FO classes:

- FO‑I — emergent conceptual crystallization  
- FO‑II — formal structural commitment  
- FO‑III — meta‑architectural transformation  

---

## ⭐ 2. FO‑I — Emergent Seed Events  
FO‑I events occur when the manifold naturally stabilizes a new pattern.

### Characteristics  
- arise from emergent dynamics  
- stabilize conceptual primitives  
- lock soft‑field gradients into repeatable behavior  
- define new basin boundaries  
- create coherent operator interactions  
- do not impose rules or constraints beyond their own stabilization  

### Function  
FO‑I events give NDH new “building blocks” — stable concepts that can be reused, referenced, or extended.

They are the foundation of NDH’s conceptual evolution.

---

## ⭐ 3. FO‑II — Formalization Events  
FO‑II events occur when NDH commits to a structure intentionally.

### Characteristics  
- represent deliberate formal decisions  
- standardize behaviors or concepts  
- define constraints or guarantees  
- establish stable interfaces or protocols  
- create commitments that persist across manifold states  

### Function  
FO‑II events transform a stabilized concept into a formal part of the architecture.  
They ensure consistency, reliability, and predictable behavior.

---

## ⭐ 4. FO‑III — Meta‑System Events  
FO‑III events occur when NDH itself evolves at a structural level.

### Characteristics  
- rare and high‑altitude  
- modify the architecture’s capabilities  
- introduce new layers, dimensions, or operator classes  
- redefine how the system organizes or governs itself  
- may alter the meaning or behavior of FO‑I and FO‑II  

### Function  
FO‑III events allow NDH to expand or restructure its architecture.  
They are the mechanism for major version‑level evolution.

---

## ⭐ 5. Classification Criteria  
A candidate FO event is classified by evaluating:

### **A. Altitude of impact**  
- conceptual → FO‑I  
- structural → FO‑II  
- architectural → FO‑III  

### **B. Nature of stabilization**  
- emergent stabilization → FO‑I  
- intentional commitment → FO‑II  
- systemic transformation → FO‑III  

### **C. Scope of effect**  
- local conceptual behavior → FO‑I  
- system‑wide rules or interfaces → FO‑II  
- architecture‑wide redefinition → FO‑III  

### **D. Persistence**  
- persists as a conceptual primitive → FO‑I  
- persists as a formal rule → FO‑II  
- persists as an architectural capability → FO‑III  

---

## ⭐ 6. FO Flow Within NDH  
FO events follow a natural progression:

1. **Emergent behavior stabilizes** → FO‑I  
2. **Stabilized behavior becomes formal** → FO‑II  
3. **Formal structures reshape the architecture** → FO‑III  

This flow ensures NDH evolves coherently, without fragmentation or drift.
