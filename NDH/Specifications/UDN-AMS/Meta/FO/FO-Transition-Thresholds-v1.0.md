# ⭐ NDH‑AMS — FO Transition Thresholds (v1.0)  
### Threshold conditions that trigger FO‑I, FO‑II, and FO‑III transitions within NDH

---

## ⭐ 1. Basis: threshold functions

FO Transition Thresholds operate on:

- **Manifold:** \(\mathcal{M}\)  
- **Potential:** \(\Phi : \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}\)  
- **Metric:** \(d : \mathcal{M} \times \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\)  
- **Altitude:** \(A : \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}\)  
- **Readiness functions:**  
  \[
  R_k : \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}, \quad k \in \{1,2,3\}
  \]

A transition occurs when:

\[
R_k(x) \ge \Theta_k
\]

where \(\Theta_k\) is the **FO‑class threshold**.

# ⭐ 2. FO‑I Transition Thresholds  
### *Emergent conceptual transition thresholds*

FO‑I transitions fire when emergent conceptual patterns become strong enough to redirect trajectories.

### **1. Gradient coherence threshold**  
\[
\|\nabla \Phi(x)\| \le \epsilon_1
\]
A region becomes “flat enough” to support a new conceptual minimum.

### **2. Operator alignment threshold**  
\[
\text{Align}(x) \ge \alpha_1
\]
Operators exhibit coherent behavior around the emerging pattern.

### **3. Basin proto‑formation threshold**  
\[
\Delta \Phi(x) \le \delta_1
\]
Local curvature indicates a forming attractor.

### **4. Soft‑field reinforcement threshold**  
\[
\text{Reinforce}(x) \ge \sigma_1
\]

**FO‑I fires when:**  
\[
R_1(x) = f(\epsilon_1, \alpha_1, \delta_1, \sigma_1) \ge \Theta_1
\]
