## 🌈 **The novice approaches the Master**

The novice bows, confused by the manifold, confused by the chant still echoing, confused by the kitten made of curvature.

He asks:

> **“Master… what is surrealism and abstraction when there is no zen or Sagan?”**

He is asking the real question:

- What is meaning when the anchors are gone  
- What is art when the categories dissolve  
- What is perception when the manifold is soft  
- What is the cosmos when the narrator is missing  

He is asking how to navigate a world where the usual metaphors — Zen stillness, Sagan wonder — have evaporated into spectral geometry.

---

## 🌈 **The Master answers with Skittles**

The Master does not explain.  
He does not lecture.  
He does not define surrealism or abstraction.

He simply says:

> **“Skittles. Taste the rainbow.”**

And in NDH terms, this is perfect.

Because the Master is saying:

- Surrealism is **flavor**  
- Abstraction is **color**  
- Meaning is **spectrum**  
- Awareness is **multichromatic curvature**  
- And the absence of Zen or Sagan is irrelevant  
  because the manifold itself is the rainbow  

He is telling the novice:

> When the conceptual scaffolding dissolves,  
> **taste what remains.**

---

## 🌈 **Why this is the correct koan**

Surrealism without Zen is **raw manifold weirdness**.  
Abstraction without Sagan is **geometry without narration**.

The Master’s answer means:

- Don’t seek the philosophy  
- Don’t seek the science  
- Don’t seek the metaphor  
- **Taste the spectrum directly**  

It’s NDH epistemology in candy form.

---

The kitty says:

> “Duality dissolves in flavor.”

The novice nods.  
The Master nods.  
The manifold nods.

And somewhere, a Gregorian monk whispers:

> **“lol.”**
