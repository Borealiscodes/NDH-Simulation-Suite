# 📘 **NDH Logic Tiles**  
### *Four Logic Modes • Human‑Portable • Offline‑Capable*

NDH uses four logic types.  
In the Low‑Tech Edition, each becomes a **single tile** with a **one‑sentence rule**.

These tiles govern how meaning moves through the simulation.

---

## 🧱 **Logic Tile Set (4 Tiles)**

- **Epistemic Logic** —  
  “Epistemic Logic turns experiences into **clear rules** and **structured ideas**.”

- **Mythic Logic** —  
  “Mythic Logic turns experiences into **stories**, **archetypes**, and **symbolic intensity**.”

- **Aesthetic Logic** —  
  “Aesthetic Logic turns experiences into **feelings**, **tone**, and **emotional coloration**.”

- **Symbolic Logic** —  
  “Symbolic Logic turns experiences into **motifs**, **patterns**, and **anchors**.”

---

# 🧭 **Routing Rules (Low‑Tech Version)**

These routing rules are the **heart** of NDH stability in low‑tech environments:

- Mythic → Aesthetic  
  “Stories soften into feelings.”

- Mythic → Symbolic  
  “Stories become symbols.”

- Symbolic ↔ Epistemic  
  “Symbols explain ideas; ideas clarify symbols.”

- Mythic → Epistemic (rare)  
  “Stories become rules only when needed.”

These can be taught orally in under 60 seconds.

---

# 🌱 **How to Use the Tiles**

### **Paper Mode**
Draw each tile as a square with its one‑sentence rule.  
Place them beside the Stability Cards and Manifold Map.

### **Oral Mode**
Teach each tile as a short phrase.  
Communities memorize them easily.

### **Community Mode**
Chalk tiles on a wall or ground.  
They become shared logic anchors.

### **Device Mode**
Store tiles as a 4‑item list in any offline notes app.

---

# 🧩 **Why These Tiles Work**
They compress NDH’s full Logic Layer into:

- four modes  
- four behaviors  
- four transitions  

This is the minimum viable logic system for **offline, low‑tech, human‑portable simulation**.
