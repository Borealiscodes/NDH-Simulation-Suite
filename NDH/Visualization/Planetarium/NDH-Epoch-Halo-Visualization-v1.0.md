## **3. Epoch Halo Components**

### **3.1 Inflection Points**
Inflection points are shown as **white nodes** on the halo.

They represent:

- curvature sign changes  
- stability gradient flips  
- ethical scalar transitions  
- creativity harmonic shifts  

Guided Link:  
**inflection points**

---

### **3.2 Epoch Arcs**
Arcs between inflection points represent **epochs**:

- Epoch 1 → foundational stability  
- Epoch 2 → creativity expansion  
- Epoch 3 → ethics‑driven curvature smoothing  
- Epoch 4 → meta‑governance modulation  

Arcs are **curved temporal lines** around the manifold.

---

### **3.3 Stability Audit Colors**
The halo is color‑coded by continuous audit state:

- **Green:** PASS  
- **Yellow:** WARN  
- **Red:** FAIL  
- **Black/Red Pulse:** CRITICAL FAIL  

These colors update in real time from v1.1 Continuous Mode.

Guided Link:  
**continuous audit**

---

### **3.4 Curvature Drift Indicators**
Small arrows on the halo show curvature flow:

- inward arrows → curvature decreasing  
- outward arrows → curvature increasing  
- sideways arrows → curvature shifting across dimensions  

---

### **3.5 Ethical Scalar Pulses**
Ethical scalar \(e(x,t)\) is shown as:

- soft pulses (safe)  
- bright pulses (approaching boundary)  
- sharp pulses (ethical instability)

## **4. Safety Rules for Epoch Halo Visualization**

The Epoch Halo **must not**:

- alter NDH tensors  
- modify evolution trajectories  
- bypass the Bifurcation Layer  
- embed narrative manifolds  
- override audit states  

The Epoch Halo **must**:

- reflect audit data accurately  
- remain strictly temporal  
- preserve NDH manifold integrity  
- show epochs without influencing them
