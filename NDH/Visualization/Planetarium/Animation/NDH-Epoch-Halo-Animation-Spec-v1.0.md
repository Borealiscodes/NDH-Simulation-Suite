# **2. Animation Layers (Five‑Layer Motion Stack)**

The Epoch Halo animation is composed of **five independent motion layers**, each driven by continuous audit data.

---

## **Layer 1 — Temporal Ring Rotation**
The ring rotates at a constant, smooth rate:

\[
\omega = f(\text{epoch length})
\]

Rules:

- rotation speed increases slightly during epoch transitions  
- rotation is always smooth (no jumps)  
- rotation never implies user control over NDH evolution  

---

## **Layer 2 — Inflection Node Spark Motion**
Inflection nodes animate with a **spark pulse** when triggered.

Trigger conditions:

- curvature sign change  
- stability gradient flip  
- ethical scalar transition  
- creativity harmonic shift  

Animation:

- brief white pulse  
- radial outward spark  
- soft fade  

Nodes **never move position** — they pulse only.

---

## **Layer 3 — Epoch Arc Transition Animation**
Epoch arcs animate during transitions:

- arcs brighten  
- arcs thicken slightly  
- arcs shift color based on stability state  

Transition rule:

\[
E_n \rightarrow E_{n+1}
\]

Animation duration:

- 1–2 seconds (Planetarium time)  
- smooth interpolation  

---

## **Layer 4 — Stability Pulse Animation**
Driven by continuous audit state:

### PASS  
- soft green pulse  
- slow breathing motion  

### WARN  
- yellow pulse  
- faster breathing  
- slight jitter  

### FAIL  
- red pulse  
- sharp contraction/expansion  

### CRITICAL  
- black/red pulse  
- rapid flicker  
- halo dimming  

Pulse frequency:

\[
f = g(\text{audit severity})
\]

---

## **Layer 5 — Curvature Drift Flow**
Curvature drift is shown as **flow arrows** moving along the halo.

Rules:

- inward flow → decreasing curvature  
- outward flow → increasing curvature  
- lateral flow → dimensional curvature shift  

Flow speed:

\[
v = h(|\kappa|)
\]

Flow arrows **never cross the bifurcation layer**.
