## ⭐ 3. HRD Safety Thresholds  
These thresholds define the maximum safe values for NDH tensors and sensory outputs.

### Tensor Safety  
- Stress tensor:  
  \[
  S_{ij} < S_{\text{safe}}
  \]
- Resonance tensor:  
  \[
  |R_{ij}| < R_{\text{safe}}
  \]
- Fold tensor:  
  \[
  F_{ij} < F_{\text{safe}}
  \]

### Sensory Safety  
- Audio peak:  
  \[
  A < A_{\text{safe}}
  \]
- Haptic amplitude:  
  \[
  H < H_{\text{safe}}
  \]
- Visual flash rate:  
  \[
  V_{\text{flash}} < V_{\text{safe}}
  \]

### Geometry Safety  
- Margin proximity:  
  \[
  P(x) < 0.9
  \]
- Corridor continuity:  
  \[
  \frac{\partial C}{\partial x} \neq 0
  \]

## ⭐ 4. HRD Ethical Constraints  
All NDH extensions must:

- respect accessibility profiles  
- avoid sensory overload  
- avoid cognitive overload  
- maintain manifold stability  
- preserve basin identity  
- preserve corridor continuity  
- avoid destructive margin interactions  
- avoid unsafe tensor spikes  
- avoid unsafe sensory intensities  

Ethics = stability + accessibility + safety.

## ⭐ 5. HRD Validation Rules  
Every NDH operation must pass HRD validation.

### Validation checks  
- **Tensor safety check**  
- **Sensory safety check**  
- **Mode transition legality check**  
- **Geometry stability check**  
- **Accessibility compliance check**  
- **Invariant preservation check**  
- **K‑space rank legality check**

## ⭐ 8. HRD Rejection Conditions  
HRD will reject any extension that:

- violates tensor safety  
- violates sensory safety  
- violates accessibility  
- violates stability ladder rules  
- violates geometry invariants  
- violates K‑space rank limits  
- fails to provide fallback behavior  
- fails to provide JDTR logging  
- fails to provide validation plan  

Rejection is logged via JDTR.
