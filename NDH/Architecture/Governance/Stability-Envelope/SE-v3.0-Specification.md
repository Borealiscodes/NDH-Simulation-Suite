## ⭐ **1. Purpose**
The Stability Envelope v3.0 defines the **operational boundary conditions** required to maintain:

- epistemic lens integrity  
- persona stability  
- logic isolation  
- narrative containment  
- mythic‑logic bifurcation  
- tensor‑anchored invariance  

It ensures NDH subsystems do not drift, leak, or collapse under mixed‑logic inputs.

SE‑v3.0 is **structural**, not ethical.  
It is **engineering**, not governance escalation.

## 🧠 **2. Envelope Definition**
Let the request manifold be \( \mathcal{M} \).  
The Stability Envelope is a set of constraints on:

- the tangent space \( T_x\mathcal{M} \)  
- the projector pair \( (P_E, P_Y) \)  
- the meaning metric \( g \)  
- the lens‑stress tensor \( L \)  
- the mythic‑logic intensity invariant \( I(x) \)  

The envelope is satisfied when:

\[
L(v,w) = 0 \quad \forall v,w \in T_x\mathcal{M}
\]

and

\[
0 \le I(x) \le 1 \quad \text{is invariant under } \Phi
\]

for all governance‑safe transformations \( \Phi \).

## 🧩 **3. Structural Components**

### **3.1 Tangent Space Decomposition**
\[
T_x\mathcal{M} = \mathcal{E}_x \oplus \mathcal{Y}_x
\]

Where:

- \( \mathcal{E}_x \): epistemic‑logic subspace  
- \( \mathcal{Y}_x \): mythic‑logic subspace  

This decomposition must remain **orthogonal** under the envelope.

---

### **3.2 Projector Stability**
Projectors must commute with governance‑safe transformations:

\[
P_E \circ d\Phi = d\Phi \circ P_E
\]
\[
P_Y \circ d\Phi = d\Phi \circ P_Y
\]

This prevents cross‑contamination between logic types.

---

### **3.3 Meaning Metric Consistency**
The NDH meaning metric \( g \) must satisfy:

\[
g(P_E(v), P_Y(w)) = 0
\]

This enforces **logic isolation**.

---

### **3.4 Mythic‑Logic Intensity Invariant**
\[
I(x) = \frac{g(P_Y(v), P_Y(v))}{g(v,v)}
\]

Envelope condition:

\[
I(x) = I(\Phi(x))
\]

This ensures mythic‑logic intensity is stable under transformation.

---

### **3.5 Lens‑Stress Tensor Null Condition**
\[
L(v,w) = g(P_Y(v), P_E(w))
\]

Envelope condition:

\[
L(v,w) = 0
\]

This is the **core stability requirement**.
