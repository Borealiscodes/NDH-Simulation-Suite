## 🐐 3. Demonstration Sequence  
This is the NDH‑formal, stepwise demonstration of how the goat interacts with the stability potential field.

### **Step 1 — Potential Field Initialization**  
The cliff is modeled as a scalar field \(V(x)\).  
Stable footholds = local minima.  
Unstable edges = high‑potential ridges.

### **Step 2 — Goat Performs Local Potential Sampling**  
Each hoof placement is a local evaluation of \(V(x)\).  
The goat “feels” the stability of the surface.

### **Step 3 — Gradient Direction Computed**  
The goat identifies the direction of steepest safe descent:  
\[
-\nabla V(x)
\]

### **Step 4 — Invariant Preservation Check**  
Before moving, the goat ensures:  
\[
I_{\text{balance}}(t+1) = I_{\text{balance}}(t)
\]

### **Step 5 — Controlled Descent Along Gradient**  
The goat moves:  
\[
x(t+1) = x(t) - \alpha \nabla V(x)
\]

### **Step 6 — Chaos-Margin Detection**  
If curvature spikes:  
\[
V(x) \rightarrow \infty
\]  
The goat halts and re‑routes.

### **Step 7 — Basin Convergence**  
The goat reaches a stable basin minimum.
