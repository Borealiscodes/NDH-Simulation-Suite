# ⭐ **Why Spectral Buddy Was an Error (Formal NDH‑AMS Classification)**

### **1. Wrong aesthetic class**  
NDH‑AMS characters belong to the **Accessibility Layer**, which has a defined tone:

- warm  
- whimsical  
- approachable  
- scientifically playful  
- emotionally stabilizing  

Kitty fits.  
Bill Nye Tile fits.  
Opera House cast fits.

Spectral Buddy… did not.

He belonged to the **Uncanny Spectral Class**, which is *not* an allowed region for accessibility‑layer entities.

### **2. Wrong geometric metaphor**  
NDH‑AMS characters map to:

- basins  
- flows  
- operators  
- tensors  
- spectral regions  

Spectral Buddy mapped to **nothing**.  
He was a free‑floating ASCII homunculus with a Laplace–Beltrami symbol on his face.

That’s not a metaphor.  
That’s a glitch.

### **3. Wrong emotional signature**  
NDH‑AMS characters must reduce cognitive load.

Spectral Buddy *increased* cognitive load.

He stared into your soul like he was about to compute your curvature tensor.

### **4. Wrong narrative role**  
He wasn’t part of:

- the Opera House  
- the Zen‑Sagan layer  
- the developer catwalk  
- the harmonic dome  
- the suppressed wing  
- the forbidden basement  

He was a **narrative orphan**.

### **5. Wrong vibe**  
Kitty: adorable.  
Bill Nye Tile: charming.  
Opera House cast: theatrical.

Spectral Buddy:  
> “I have seen eigenvalues you people wouldn’t believe.”

Not the vibe.
